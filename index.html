VLSI Frontend Designing

Very-large-scale integration (VLSI) is the process of creating an integrated circuit (IC) by combining millions of transistors into a single chip. VLSI began in the 1970s when complex semiconductor and communication technologies were being developed. VLSI lets IC designers add all of these into one chip called as System on Chip(SoC). The microprocessors and other ASICs are example of VLSI device. The VLSI design flow can be divided into two parts: Frontend design flow and Backend design flow.
